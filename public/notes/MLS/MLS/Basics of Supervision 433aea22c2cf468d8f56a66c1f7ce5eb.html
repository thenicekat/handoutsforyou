Objectives of Supervision

  • Anything a manager does outside doing work → supervision
  • Every manager is a supervisor → to immediate subordinate
  • Carrying out a induction or a orientation program
  • Meet targets and make best use of resources
  • Maintain/lead an effective workforce → They should feel motivated and want to overcome issues
  • Training so that the employee can produce quality output
  • Discipline and safety of the employee
  • Industry relation to ensure an environment which will lead to no issues
  • Manager role counts supervisor roles also

Supervisors (not managers)

  • Raw material → process → output
  • As a supervisor → responsible for quality, quantity, cost and flow
  • Responsible for layout of the office, plant or factory
  • React optimum utilization point
  • Maintenance → using right methods
  • These overlap with managers
  • Dean is supervisor for ADs and ADs are supervising bodies

Tools of Supervision

  • Organization
    • Every employee should know whom they report to
    • They should know clearly their line of authority
  • Planning
    • His own work as well as for his subordinates
    • Methods for doing the job
    • Standards, time limits
  • Orders and instructions
    • Could be oral or written but should be clear and have clarity
    • Lower level instruction → you need to repeat it again and again
    • Giving instructions only gets work done
    • You need to understand the other person
  • Checking and inspecting
    • Checking should be periodical and not at the end
    • Safety regulations
    • Right method used
  • Coordination
    • How to get coordination from everyone in the department
    • His or her ability to conduct meetings
  • Manpower
    • Ensure workload is balanced of the employees
  • Professional Knowledge
    • Stay upto date with knowledge and keep his employees upto date
  • Work Experience
    • Learning from other’s experience
    • Supervisor should share his experience with the employees
  • Company Policies
    • Supervisor should have clear idea and interpretation of the policies
    • They should be able to defend it and know the intent behind it
    • Actual reason for PS2 → in 1982 they couldn’t put everyone in BITS hostels
  • Job Improvements
    • Ensure that any employee who is doing his job in your domain
    • Improve productivity, reduce wastage, reduce accidents
    • Job satisfaction
  • Delegation
  • Training
    • Ensure that your subordinates carry out the task, safely

Relational Responsibilities

  • Subordinates
    • Listen to feedback
    • Train them
  • Superiors
    • Making no excuses about work
    • Keep boss informed
    • Assist his or her work in his workload
  • Associates
    • Understand your peer group
    • Be cooperative

Problems of Supervision

  • Uncooperative employees
    • Attitude problems
  • Orders carried out improperly
  • Problems with materials
    • You will have quality control issues
  • Problems with machines
  • Defective work
    • Identify cause of defective work
  • Absenteeism
    • Too many leaves by an employee
    • Talk to the employee and find out his situation
  • Accidents
    • Any accident that is taking place should be recorded
    • Should be investigated
    • What is the cause of the accident
    • People → Poor move by the employee
  • Indifferent employees
    • Difficult group of employees to deal with
    • Need to give feedback and close monitor
    • Close supervision
    • Refer to a senior employee
